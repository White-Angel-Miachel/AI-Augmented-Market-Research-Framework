### 3. Key Trends

#### 1. Widespread Adoption with Shallow Deployment

Nearly all organizations (88%) use AI, but most remain in early pilot phases rather than achieving enterprise-wide scaled deployment. Only one-third have begun scaling AI programs across the organization.

**Key Data Points**
- 88% report regular AI use in at least one business function (up from 78% in 2024)
- 65% have not yet begun scaling AI across the enterprise
- Larger companies ($5B+ revenue) are far more likely to scale: nearly 50% vs. 29% for companies <$100M revenue
- Half of respondents use AI in three or more functions, up from 33% in 2021

#### 2. AI Agents Gaining Traction but Not Yet Scaled

AI agents (systems capable of planning and executing multiple steps in workflows) are generating significant interest, with nearly two-thirds of organizations experimenting. However, deployment remains limited to one or two functions for most.

**Key Data Points**
- 62% of respondents say their organizations are at least experimenting with AI agents
- 23% report scaling an agentic AI system somewhere in their enterprise
- In any given business function, no more than 10% are scaling AI agents
- IT and knowledge management have highest agent adoption; manufacturing and supply chain lowest

#### 3. Innovation Impact Exceeds Financial Impact

Organizations report strong qualitative benefits from AI (innovation, customer satisfaction, competitive differentiation), but enterprise-level EBIT impact remains limited. Cost benefits are concentrated in specific functions.

**Key Data Points**
- 64% say AI is enabling innovation at their organizations
- 39% report any level of EBIT impact from AI (most report <5% of EBIT)
- 45% report improved customer satisfaction; 45% report improved competitive differentiation
- Cost decreases most common in software engineering (56% report decrease), manufacturing (51%), and IT (49%)

#### 4. High Performers Pursue Transformation, Not Just Efficiency

The elite cohort of AI high performers (6% of respondents) distinguish themselves by setting growth and innovation objectives alongside efficiency goals, and by fundamentally redesigning workflows rather than layering AI onto existing processes.

**Key Data Points**
- High performers 3.6x more likely to pursue transformative business change
- 80% of all respondents set efficiency as AI objective, but high performers also set growth (50%) and innovation (79%)
- High performers 2.8x more likely to fundamentally redesign workflows (55% vs. 20%)
- High performers 3x more likely to have senior leaders who demonstrate ownership of AI initiatives

#### 5. Risk Mitigation Improving but Consequences Remain Common

Organizations are increasingly addressing AI risks, with the average number of mitigated risks doubling from 2 in 2022 to 4 in 2025. However, over half of organizations have experienced at least one negative consequence from AI.

**Key Data Points**
- 51% of respondents say their organizations have seen at least one negative consequence from AI
- 30% report experiencing AI inaccuracy—the most common negative consequence
- Average number of AI risks being mitigated: 4 (up from 2 in 2022)
- High performers more likely to experience negative consequences but also more likely to mitigate them
